Gold Digger

Privacy Policy · Last updated April 19, 2026

Overview

Gold Digger is a Chrome extension and web app that helps vinyl record collectors find the cheapest combination of sellers for their Discogs wantlist. This policy explains how the extension handles data.

Data We Do Not Collect

The Gold Digger extension does not:

  • Collect, store, or transmit any personal information to third parties
  • Track browsing history or activity outside of discogs.com marketplace pages
  • Share any data with advertisers or analytics services
  • Sell or monetize any user data

Permissions Used

cookies

The extension reads your existing Discogs.com session cookies solely to authenticate marketplace page requests on your behalf. This allows it to fetch seller listings that require a logged-in Discogs account. Your cookies are never stored beyond the current sync session and are never transmitted to any server other than discogs.com.

storage

The extension uses chrome.storage.local to store your Gold Digger server URL, Discogs username, and sync progress state locally on your device. This data never leaves your browser and is only used to remember your settings between sessions.

tabs

The tabs permission is used solely to open the Gold Digger web app in a new tab when you click "Open Gold Digger" from the extension popup.

host permissions (discogs.com, stream.ronautradio.la)

The extension accesses discogs.com marketplace pages to read seller listing information (seller name, price, condition, ships-from country) for records in your Discogs wantlist. It accesses stream.ronautradio.la to send this listing data to the Gold Digger optimizer, which calculates the cheapest cart combination.

Data Transmission

Scraped seller listing data (seller username, price, condition, ships-from) is sent to the Gold Digger server at stream.ronautradio.la, which you operate or have authorized access to. No data is sent to any other server.
